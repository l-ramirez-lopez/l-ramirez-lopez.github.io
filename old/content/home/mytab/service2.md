I am Agronomist by training but through my Master and PhD studies I became a 
data scientist...

<!--more-->

I hold a degree in Agronomic Engineering, a Masters degree (remote sensing) and 
Doctoral (_magna cum laude_) in Soil Science with focus on machine learning.

During my master (2007-2009) at the remote sensing laboratories at the 
University of São Paulo (Brazil), I carried out research on the use of soil 
sensing and machine learning tools to study the spatial distribution of soils 
in the landscape. In 2013 I completed my PhD dissertation at the Univeristy of 
Tübingen in the context of an European Union (EU) founded project iSOIL - 
Interactions between soil related sciences – Linking geophysics, soil science 
and digital soil mapping. During the last year of my PhD I also worked at the
Université catholique de Louvain (Belgium) as a researcher in a project in 
which the main goal was to develop techniques for three dimensional soil organic 
carbon (SOC) monitoring at the regional scale. In 2013 I moved to Switzerland
to work as postdoctoral scientist at the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology 
(ETH). My research at ETH was devoted to the development of accurate spatial 
models of soil properties relevant to hydro-pedological processes for the whole 
forested area of Switzerland using legacy soil data, spatially comprehensive 
environmental covariates and digital soil mapping methods. 

I have published numerous research papers and software programs (and I am also 
an active open source software developer).
